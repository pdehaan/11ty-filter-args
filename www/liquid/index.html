Testing filter arguments in liquidjs@6.4.3

No arguments

value='The Quick Brown Fox Jumps Over the Lazy Cat';

One argument

value='The Quick Brown Fox Jumps Over the Lazy Cat';
two='OnE';

Two arguments

value='The Quick Brown Fox Jumps Over the Lazy Cat';
two='oNe';
three='TwO';

Two arguments, both undefined

value='The Quick Brown Fox Jumps Over the Lazy Cat';
two=undefined;
three=undefined;

Two arguments, both optional (with defaults)

value='The Quick Brown Fox Jumps Over the Lazy Cat';
two='SeVeN';
three={ age: 'old (default)' };

Note that passing an object literal as a filter argument isn't supported in liquidjs@6.4.3. In order to pass an object as an argument, we can define it in the frontmatter or use a global data file. 

value='The Quick Brown Fox Jumps Over the Lazy Cat';
two='SeVeN';
three={ age: 'old (default)', name: 'Paul (frontmatter)' };
